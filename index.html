Welcome to Discovering Spain

My Story

Traveling to Spain is like opening a living encyclopedia of culture. From its grand cathedrals to golden beaches, from Goya’s art to Iniesta’s magic, this country blends history, flavors, and emotions. What always drew me in was its passion for football—an unconditional love felt in every square, bar, and stadium.

15 Must-Do Experiences

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium Tour

Walking through the players’ tunnel gave me chills. It felt like stepping into the footsteps of legends before a roaring crowd.

Watching El Clásico Live

I got tickets to Real Madrid vs Barcelona—it was wild! The energy, chants, and rivalry made it unforgettable.

Spotify Camp Nou (Barcelona)

Though under renovation, the museum was open and full of historic shirts, trophies, and the spirit of Barça.

Benito Villamarín Stadium

Real Betis’ stadium is pure passion. Fans cheer nonstop and the green-and-white colors light up the entire experience.

Wanda Metropolitano Stadium

Atlético’s stadium is modern and easy to tour. You feel the club’s grit and spirit around every corner.

Malagueta Beach

Sun, fried fish, and blue sea—perfect combo! The beach vibe was chill, local, and full of joy.

Oceanogràfic Aquarium

Sharks, penguins, and dolphins—all in one place. It’s the largest aquarium in Europe, and every zone amazed me.

Prado Museum

Velázquez and Goya moved me to tears again. Art here speaks of centuries of emotion, beauty, and struggle.

Barcelona Zoo

A peaceful afternoon watching elephants and gorillas. Great place for animal lovers and photography fans.

Parque del Oeste Viewpoint

A sunset with city and stadium views in the distance. One of those quiet corners that captures the soul of Madrid.

Where I Stayed

NYX Hotel Madrid: Modern design with a football vibe and a rooftop terrace for sunsets.

Hotel de Londres y de Inglaterra: A room with a sea view made every night magical.

Catalonia Rigoletto: Just a short walk from Camp Nou, with excellent service and breakfast.

Hotel Casa 1800 (Seville): An Andalusian palace with interior patios and rich history in every corner.

Travel Tips

Traveling responsibly can be easy and rewarding. Here are my best tips, based on my own experience.:

  • You have to plan ahead: stadiums, museums, and tours often sell out quickly. Trust me, it saved me from missing out on key experiences.
  • You should carry a reusable water bottle: I used mine every day—in train stations, museums, and long walks.
  • Why don’t you rent a bike in Seville or San Sebastián? It’s fun, cheap, and good for the environment. I felt more connected to the streets and people this way.
  • Try to eat in local restaurants instead of chains. You’ll get tastier, more authentic food, and you’ll be supporting small businesses.
  • If I were you, I would use public transportation. Metro systems in cities like Madrid and Barcelona are fast, clean, and reduce your carbon footprint.
  • Don’t forget to bring your own reusable bag for snacks, souvenirs, or groceries. I used mine almost every day.
  • It’s a good idea to carry a small trash bag when hiking or visiting beaches. I kept one in my backpack just in case.

These small actions helped me travel more sustainably. And honestly, they made me feel like I was giving something back to the places that gave me so much.
